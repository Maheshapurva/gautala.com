About Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary

Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. It is home to a variety of wildlife, including tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and many species of birds and reptiles.

Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the sanctuary, where they can observe wildlife in its natural habitat and learn about conservation efforts to protect these species.

Getting There

The sanctuary is located approximately 60 kilometers from Aurangabad and can be reached by car or bus. The nearest airport is Aurangabad Airport, which is approximately 65 kilometers away.

Visitors should plan to arrive early in the morning or late in the afternoon for the best chance of seeing wildlife.
