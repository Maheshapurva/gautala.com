About Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary

Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. It covers an area of approximately 259 sq km and was established in 1986 to protect the biodiversity of the region.

The sanctuary is home to a variety of animals, including tigers, leopards, sloth bears, wild boars, hyenas, and many species of birds and reptiles.

Animals at Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary

Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary is known for its impressive population of animals, which are the main attraction for visitors. Other large mammals that can be seen in the sanctuary include leopards, sloth bears, Indian bison, and sambar deer.

There are also many smaller animals and birds living in the sanctuary, such as porcupines, pangolins, jungle cats, Indian hares, Indian rock pythons, Indian cobra, vipers, owls, eagles, and many more.

Flora and Fauna

Gautala Wildlife Sanctuary has a rich variety of flora and fauna due to its location in the Sahyadri ranges. The sanctuary is dominated by deciduous forest and has a mix of dry and moist deciduous vegetation. It is also home to various species of medicinal plants and trees.

The sanctuary is also known for its rich birdlife, with over 200 species of birds recorded in the region. Visitors can spot the Indian Pitta, Chestnut-bellied Nuthatch, and many species of woodpeckers, owls, and flycatchers among others.
